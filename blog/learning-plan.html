Learning Plan

1. Long-Term Goal / Career Pathway

My long-term career goal is to work as a software developer, specializing in full-stack development. I want to become proficient in both front-end and back-end development, with a focus on building impactful, user-centric applications. In the long run, I also see myself contributing to the tech community through open-source projects and potentially mentoring future developers.

I aim to be a present parent and work from home as much as possible to be there to watch every milestone. The ability to work from anywhere! TRAVEL! Be with my whānau as much as possible. Financial Freedom!!!!

Leading a team of developers, designers, and marketers to create a product that people love. Building a successful startup can bring personal fulfillment and the excitement of seeing an idea turn into something impactful! DREAMS ARE BIG!

2. Strengths and Limitations in Learning

Strengths

  • Analytical Thinking: I have strong problem-solving skills, which help me break down complex problems into manageable pieces.
  • Persistence: I tend to persist through challenges and I’m not easily discouraged, which helps me stay focused on my learning goals.
  • Curiosity: I love learning new things, and my curiosity helps me absorb new concepts and keep up-to-date with the latest technology trends.

Limitations

  • Overthinking: Sometimes, I tend to overthink problems, which can slow down my progress and decision-making.
  • Imposter Syndrome: At times, I struggle with self-doubt, especially when I encounter something new that feels outside of my comfort zone.
  • Time Management: Juggling multiple tasks can sometimes overwhelm me, leading to procrastination or delayed progress.

3. Skills to Develop at Dev Academy

While at Dev Academy, I would like to develop the following non-technical skills:

  • Collaboration and Communication: I want to improve my ability to work effectively in teams, especially in communicating ideas and concepts clearly with others.
  • Resilience in Learning: I aim to develop greater resilience to handle setbacks and challenges that arise during the learning process.
  • Time Management: I would like to improve my ability to manage my time efficiently, particularly when balancing coding tasks, projects, and life responsibilities.
  • Emotional Intelligence: Strengthening my emotional intelligence will help me navigate interpersonal relationships and collaborate better with peers, facilitators, and industry professionals.

4. Commitment to Managing Workload

To manage my workload effectively, I commit to the following:

  • Working Productively and Safely with Others: I will actively participate in group discussions and projects, listen to others' perspectives. I’ll respect the work-life boundaries of fellow learners & facilitators, ensuring our interactions are respectful, professional, and safe.
  • When Things Build Up: If I find myself feeling overwhelmed, I’ll take a break to clear my mind, practice mindfulness, have a nap or swim, and then review the situation to prioritize tasks. I will seek support from my peers or facilitators if needed.
  • Seeking Help in a Timely Way: I will make sure to reach out for help as soon as I recognize that I need it. Asking questions during class, sending a message to a facilitator, or collaborating with fellow learners. I will not hesitate to utilize online resources and the available community support if necessary.
  • Expectations from Facilitation Team: I expect the facilitation team to provide clear, actionable feedback on my work, especially in areas where I can improve. I also expect them to be approachable and available for one-on-one support, especially when I’m struggling with a specific topic or task.

5. Scheduling Information

I have the following commitments outside of the program:

  • Whānau Commitments: I have a whānau commitment that requires me to be available for school drop-offs in the mornings. This means I will need to plan my study schedule accordingly, possibly beginning my day later but remaining fully committed to the work after that time.
  • Other Obligations: I also have occasional commitments taking care of my grandparents, or if my child gets sick or has appointments.

Block-Out Times:

  • Monday to Friday: 7:30 AM - 9:00 AM (Kohanga drop-offs).
  • 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM (MOVE MY BODY – Gym or walk)
  • Weekends: Occasionally, I am involved in family activities or community engagements, so I may need flexibility for about 2-4 hours on Saturdays or Sundays.

I will plan my learning activities in the evenings or in scheduled breaks, ensuring a balance between personal responsibilities and my academic goals.
