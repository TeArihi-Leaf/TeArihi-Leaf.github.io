Ethical Decision: Weighing Values and Reflection

In a past situation, I had to make a decision about whether or not to speak up about a colleague’s actions that I believed were not aligned with the company's values. On one hand, I felt the need to uphold integrity, honesty, and transparency, which are principles that guide my actions. On the other hand, I valued connection and the desire to maintain a harmonious team environment, and I feared that confronting the issue might cause tension or even damage the relationship with my colleague.

I took some time to reflect on my core values, authenticity and connection. I recognised that maintaining my integrity was essential, and I needed to act in alignment with my true self, even if it was uncomfortable. Ultimately, I decided to speak to my colleague privately, sharing my concerns and offering support for any challenges they might be facing. This approach not only allowed me to honor my personal values but also preserved the relationship by showing empathy and respect.

Reflecting now, I am proud of my decision. The conversation allowed us to clear the air, and while it was a difficult discussion, it was necessary for both personal growth and the health of the team. I continue to value open communication and integrity, and this experience reinforced the importance of having difficult but necessary conversations to maintain both authenticity and connection.

Whakapapa (Ancestry and Connection)

A central value in Māori culture is whakapapa, which links us to our ancestors and the land. This understanding of interconnectedness has instilled in me a deep respect for family, history, and heritage. I value the stories passed down through generations, and I feel a sense of duty to honor and carry forward the wisdom and traditions of those who came before me. This connection to my Māori roots provides a strong foundation for my identity and guides my decisions.

Manaakitanga (Hospitality and Care)

The concept of manaakitanga, or hospitality, is integral to Māori culture. It teaches the importance of caring for others, showing kindness, and offering support to those in need. This value has shaped the way I interact with people in my life, as I aim to be someone who is generous with my time, resources, and love. It encourages me to be empathetic and to nurture my relationships with others, creating a sense of belonging and mutual respect.

Aroha (Love and Compassion)

Aroha, meaning love and compassion, is at the core of Māori culture. It reflects the idea that love is a guiding force that transcends any challenges or hardships we may face. This value has encouraged me to approach life with an open heart, to show compassion, and to act with empathy. Aroha reminds me to prioritize kindness in all my interactions and to offer support and understanding, especially in times of difficulty.

Rangatiratanga (Leadership and Self-Determination)

Māori culture also emphasizes rangatiratanga, which is about leadership, self-determination, and autonomy. This value encourages me to be confident in my abilities and to take responsibility for my actions and decisions. It’s about leading with integrity and ensuring that I am contributing to the well-being of my community and my people. Rangatiratanga reminds me to stand firm in my identity and to advocate for myself and others in a way that respects my values and culture.

whānau (family) and iwi (tribe), have also played a significant role in shaping my values and identity. They have taught me about resilience, respect, and the importance of community. My whānau has always emphasized the significance of aroha, manaakitanga, and tikanga (customs) in everyday life. Through their example, I’ve learned that true leadership is about serving others, not seeking personal gain.

Strengths Supporting My Learning Journey

One of my greatest strengths is insight and guidance. I have a natural ability to see patterns in situations and offer advice or guidance that others may not have thought of. This strength will be incredibly valuable as I navigate my learning journey, allowing me to approach challenges from a unique perspective and find creative solutions. I also possess strong listening skills, which allow me to understand others deeply. This will help me collaborate with peers, absorb diverse viewpoints, and incorporate constructive feedback into my learning.

Limitations and How They Might Affect My Learning Journey

One limitation I’ve noticed is my tendency to be overly introspective at times. While reflection is essential for personal growth, I can sometimes overthink or hesitate when making decisions. This might slow my progress in situations that require quick action or when I need to make snap judgments in my learning journey or career development. Additionally, my natural inclination to want to connect with others may sometimes lead me to focus on maintaining relationships at the expense of my own boundaries or needs. I must actively remind myself to balance connection with self-care to avoid burnout.

Working Productively with Others: Overcoming Tension

I once worked on a team project where there was significant tension between myself and a friend. Our working styles were very different, and I found it difficult to make progress with the project due to the resistance I was encountering. At the time, I tried to approach the situation with patience and empathy, trying to understand where my friend was coming from and offering compromise.

One strategy I tried was to sit down and communicate with my friend about the challenges we were facing. I asked for their input on how we could better collaborate and actively listened to their concerns. While this approach led to some improvements in communication, it didn't fully resolve the tension, as my friend was not fully open to compromise.

Core Values: What Guides Me

The values that guide me are authenticity, connection, wisdom, and self-care. I have always valued living authentically, being true to myself regardless of external pressures. This authenticity extends to my relationships, where I prioritise building genuine, meaningful connections, even if that means not fitting into traditional molds.

Strengths: What Makes Me Shine

I believe my insight and guidance have always been a strength. Whether it's helping friends work through difficult situations or providing perspectives others might overlook, I’ve been able to offer support that truly resonates with others. Additionally, my problem-solving skills have allowed me to find creative solutions in complex situations, especially when I trust my intuition.

Practical Insights for Living Authentically

  • Embrace Your Intuition: Trust your gut, even when it feels counterintuitive. I've learned that following my inner wisdom often leads to the best outcomes.
  • Honor Your Boundaries: It's okay to say "no" when necessary, and I’ve learned that creating space for rest and rejuvenation helps me stay engaged and energized.
  • Seek Meaningful Connections: Instead of trying to meet everyone’s expectations, I focus on nurturing deep, authentic relationships. These connections are what truly sustain me.

My Ongoing Journey

Reflecting on my journey so far, I am proud of how far I’ve come. I continue to learn about myself, my values, and my strengths, and I am excited about the ongoing process of self-discovery. This is not a journey with a definitive endpoint but rather one of continuous growth and understanding. Embrace who you are and trust in the process of becoming your most authentic self. Let’s continue learning, growing, and supporting each other on this shared journey.
