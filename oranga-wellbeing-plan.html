Oranga / Wellbeing Plan — Bootcamp Update

I updated my wellbeing / oranga plan during Bootcamp and added it to my learning blog. Foundations gave me a solid starting point, but Bootcamp showed me I needed more flexibility, compassion, and balance to move through the mahi as a Māori wāhine, a māmā, and a future founder.

Tinana (Physical)

  • Daily movement: gym when energy allows, walks after drop-offs, or stretching on low days.
  • Kai & wai: lean lower-carb for less bloating and steadier energy; hydrate throughout the day.
  • Rest: aim for 7–8 hours sleep, guilt-free early nights when needed.

Hinengaro (Mental)

  • Journaling: regular reflections to process thoughts, bugs, and breakthroughs.
  • Mindfulness: short breaks, breathing, or karakia to reset during study blocks.
  • Positive kōrero: replace “I can’t” with “I’m still learning.”

Wairua (Spiritual)

  • Morning karakia or gratitude to start the day intentionally.
  • Connection with te taiao: sunlight, whenua, fresh air for wairua reset.
  • Protecting energy: say no when overwhelmed, prioritising inner peace.

Whānau (Social / Emotional)

  • Whānau time: prioritise quality time with my son and partner.
  • Lean on support systems: facilitators, classmates, and loved ones.
  • Celebrate wins together: milestones, breakthroughs, and progress.

He Whakaaro (Reflection)

Updating this plan mid-Bootcamp helped me reconnect with my values and keep my hauora balanced. Some weeks I was consistent; others I reset and tried again. This plan isn’t about perfection — it’s about awareness and self-respect. It represents who I’m becoming: a grounded Māori wāhine in tech, balancing mahi, motherhood, and self-growth with aroha and purpose.
