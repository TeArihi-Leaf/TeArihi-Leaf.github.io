1. Long-Term Goal / Career Pathway

My long-term career goal is to work as a software developer, specializing in full-stack development. I want to become proficient in both front-end and back-end development, focusing on building impactful, user-centric applications. In the long run, I also see myself contributing to the tech community through open-source projects and potentially mentoring future developers.

Beyond technical expertise, I aim to be a present parent, work from home as much as possible to witness every milestone, and travel. I dream of having the ability to work from anywhere while being with my whānau as much as possible. Financial freedom and leading a team of developers, designers, and marketers to create products that people love is part of my vision. Ultimately, building a successful startup will bring personal fulfillment, with the excitement of seeing an idea turn into something impactful. DREAMS ARE BIG!

2. Strengths and Limitations in Learning

Strengths

  • Analytical Thinking: Strong problem-solving skills that help break down complex problems into manageable pieces.
  • Persistence: I persist through challenges and stay focused on my learning goals, not easily discouraged.
  • Curiosity: I love learning new things, helping me stay up-to-date with the latest technology trends.

Limitations

  • Overthinking: Tending to overthink problems, which can slow down my progress and decision-making.
  • Imposter Syndrome: Self-doubt occasionally arises, especially when encountering something outside my comfort zone.
  • Time Management: Juggling multiple tasks sometimes leads to procrastination or delayed progress.

3. Skills to Develop at Dev Academy

While at Dev Academy, I would like to develop the following non-technical skills:

  • Collaboration and Communication: Improve my ability to work effectively in teams and communicate ideas clearly.
  • Resilience in Learning: Develop greater resilience to handle setbacks and challenges during the learning process.
  • Time Management: Improve my ability to manage time efficiently, balancing coding tasks, projects, and life responsibilities.
  • Emotional Intelligence: Strengthen my emotional intelligence to navigate interpersonal relationships and collaborate effectively with peers and facilitators.

4. Commitment to Managing Workload

To manage my workload effectively, I commit to the following:

  • Working Productively and Safely with Others: I will actively participate in group discussions, listen to others' perspectives, and respect the work-life boundaries of fellow learners and facilitators.
  • When Things Build Up: If I feel overwhelmed, I’ll take a break, practice mindfulness, and then prioritize tasks. I will seek support when needed.
  • Seeking Help in a Timely Way: I will reach out for help as soon as I recognize I need it, utilizing class discussions, messages to facilitators, and online resources.
  • Expectations from Facilitation Team: I expect clear, actionable feedback and one-on-one support when struggling with specific topics.
